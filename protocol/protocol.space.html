Space Protocol

Protocol Book · Chapter 02

Space is not only where things exist. Space is how meaning begins.

1. Literary Introduction

Before language became grammar, there was space. A child reaches upward before speaking “up.” Water falls before anyone invents the word “down.” The sky surrounds the earth before any civilization names it.

Every language is written differently, but every human body lives inside the same space. Meaning begins not with words, but with movement inside space.

在语言成为语法之前，世界已经拥有空间。孩子在学会“上”之前，已经知道伸手。 水在学会名字之前，已经向下流。天空包围大地，远早于任何一种语言。

2. Definition

What is Space?

Space is not merely physical location. Within the Semantic Protocol, Space is the relational structure that organizes meaning.

Objects, actions, emotions, and concepts are interpreted according to their positions, directions, distance, and containment.

Meaning exists because semantic objects occupy space.

空间不仅仅是物理位置。在 Semantic Protocol 中，Space 是组织意义的关系结构。 对象、动作、情感和概念，都通过位置、方向、距离和内外关系获得意义。

4. Etymology

English space comes from Latin spatium, meaning room, distance, interval, or extent.

French espace also comes from Latin spatium.

Chinese 空间 combines 空, meaning empty, and 间, meaning interval or between.

“空间”由“空”和“间”组成。空，表示可容纳；间，表示间隔与关系。 因此，空间不是虚无，而是意义关系可以发生的场域。

5. Unilanguage Interpretation

Traditional linguistics often treats space as a background for language. Unilanguage reverses this perspective.

Language does not simply exist in space. Language exists because semantic space already organizes meaning.

Words are compressed descriptions of spatial relations. Different languages encode the same spatial structures through different sounds and symbols.

Semantic correspondence is often more fundamental than lexical correspondence.

6. Semantic Rules

Rule 1 · Meaning begins with spatial distinction

No space, no object. No object, no relation. No relation, no meaning.

Rule 2 · Every semantic object occupies a position

Sky, earth, human, tree, house, and body all carry spatial positions.

Rule 3 · Direction creates semantic change

Up, down, inside, outside, near, and far are not only physical directions. They are semantic operators.

Rule 4 · Containment creates category

Body, house, city, memory, book, and language can all be understood through inside/outside structure.

Rule 5 · Distance creates relation

Near, far, approach, leave, return, belong, and exile all come from distance transformation.

7. Spatial Directions

In Semantic Protocol, direction is not decorative. Direction is a basic semantic force.

  • UP: rise, growth, sky, hope, spirit
  • DOWN: fall, depth, earth, body, foundation
  • IN: inner world, memory, self, protection
  • OUT: expression, expansion, openness, freedom

8. Spatial Metaphors

9. Case Study · Sora, Sky, and World Model

The name Sora provides an interesting illustration of semantic space. In Japanese, sora means sky.

The sky is not merely an object above us. It represents openness, height, freedom, imagination, and possibility.

In modern AI, a world model does not merely generate words. It attempts to reconstruct spatial relations, physical interactions, and causal structures.

Space becomes the bridge between imagination and reality.

Sora 可以作为一个空间隐喻案例。天空不仅是物理空间，也是自由、开放、想象和可能性的象征。 当 AI 从语言模型走向世界模型，空间不再只是背景，而成为理解现实规则的基础。

11. Reflection

Before there is a dictionary, there is direction. Before there is grammar, there is distance. Before there is language, there is space.

To understand language, first understand space.

在词典之前，是方向。 在语法之前，是距离。 在语言之前，是空间。 理解语言，应先理解空间。
