Unilanguage Translation Protocol Laboratory

Testing how “sky” changes across language, metaphor, and semantic space.

1. Translation Task

Translate and evaluate the word sky across English, Chinese, French, Japanese, and Unilanguage semantic mapping.

本页任务：测试 sky 在英语、汉语、法语、日语以及 Unilanguage 语义映射中的翻译方式。

2. Literal Translation

Language Literal Translation Notes
English sky upper visible space above the earth
Chinese 天空 direct everyday translation
French ciel sky / heaven
Japanese sora 空, sky

3. Semantic Translation

The word sky can be translated differently depending on semantic context.

Context Chinese Translation Reason
Daily speech 天空 basic visible sky
Classical / literary 天 / 昊 / 苍穹 vastness, height, cosmic feeling
Religious / symbolic 天 / 天堂 heavenly or spiritual meaning
Spatial metaphor 盖 / 穹顶 sky as cover or dome over the earth
AI / imagination 想象空间 / 世界模型 sky as open semantic space

4. Unilanguage Mapping

In Unilanguage, sky is interpreted through spatial direction and metaphor. 

          SKY
           ↑
        upper space
           ↑
   height / openness / cover
           ↑
      天空 · 昊 · 盖

Proposed Mapping: sky ↔ 天空 / 昊 / 盖

sky 的核心语义不是一个单词，而是一个空间结构： 上方、开放、覆盖、广大、自由。

5. Mapping Justification

The mapping is justified by spatial perception.

Human beings experience the sky as the upper space above the body and the earth. Therefore, sky naturally carries the semantic direction UP.

The sky also visually covers the earth like a dome. This allows the metaphorical mapping:

sky → upper cover → 盖

In Chinese literary language, 昊 expresses the vast and bright sky. It expands the meaning from physical space to cosmic imagination.

sky 与“盖”的关系不是严格词源关系，而是空间隐喻关系。 天空像一个巨大的盖子覆盖大地，因此可以形成 sky ↔ 盖 的 Unilanguage 映射。

6. Evidence

Classical Chinese Evidence

In Chinese tradition, 天 is both the physical sky and the cosmic order. 昊 often refers to the vast sky in literary expression.

Cross-language Evidence

French ciel can mean both sky and heaven. Japanese sora means sky and may evoke openness or freedom in cultural interpretation.

Spatial Evidence

Across languages, sky is frequently connected with height, openness, distance, divinity, freedom, weather, and imagination.

7. AI Comparison

A normal translation system may translate sky simply as 天空.

A semantic translation system should decide whether the context requires:

  • 天空 — literal sky
  • 天 — classical or symbolic sky
  • 昊 — vast literary sky
  • 苍穹 — poetic sky / firmament
  • 盖 / 穹顶 — spatial metaphor
  • 想象空间 — symbolic or AI-related interpretation

UTP evaluates not only whether a translation is correct, but whether it preserves semantic space.

8. Benchmark

Test Sentence Literal Translation Semantic Translation

9. Result

The best translation of sky depends on semantic layer.
